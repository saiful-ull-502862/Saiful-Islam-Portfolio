About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, specializing in Finite Element Analysis, Soft Materials, and Computational Biomechanics. With 4+ years of hands-on experience, I develop advanced multi-scale computational frameworks to solve complex challenges in cartilage mechanics and osteoarthritis research.

My research uniquely bridges mechanical engineering and bioengineering, integrating constitutive modeling, image analysis, and AI/ML approaches in ABAQUS and Python. I have authored peer-reviewed publications, presented at international conferences (SB3C, ORS, CMBBE), and developed novel methodologies for tissue characterization that advance understanding of disease progression.

I am actively seeking industrial internship opportunities in R&D, simulation engineering, or computational mechanics roles where I can apply my expertise in FEA, material modeling, and data-driven analysis to deliver innovative engineering solutions.
