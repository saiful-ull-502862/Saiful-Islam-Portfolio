Leaky Rayleigh Wave Modeling for Early-Age Concrete

January 2024 - August 2024
Tran's Lab, Michigan Tech
Dr. Quang Tran
#FEA #NDT #Multiphysics #Civil Engineering #Ultrasonics

Project Overview

This research develops innovative non-contact ultrasonic techniques using air-coupled leaky Rayleigh waves for assessing early-stage concrete properties. By integrating finite element simulations with experimental validation, we enable real-time monitoring of concrete curing and strength development without physical contact, addressing a critical need in construction quality control.

Non-Destructive Innovation

Air-coupled ultrasonics eliminate the need for physical coupling agents, enabling continuous monitoring of concrete from initial pour through final cure without interfering with the hydration process.

Research Objectives

  • Develop air-coupled finite element model for leaky Rayleigh wave propagation
  • Characterize wave behavior in early-age concrete (0-72 hours)
  • Correlate wave parameters with concrete mechanical properties
  • Validate computational predictions against experimental measurements
  • Create predictive models for strength development monitoring
  • Contribute to NSF proposal on multiphysics modeling of concrete evolution

Methodology

Computational Modeling

  • Multiphysics FEA: Coupled acoustic-structural simulations in COMSOL/ABAQUS
  • Domain: Air-concrete interface with absorbing boundaries
  • Wave Generation: Piezoelectric transducer modeling for ultrasonic excitation
  • Material Evolution: Time-dependent properties modeling cement hydration
  • Parameter Studies: Frequency, angle, and mix design variations

Experimental Setup

  • Air-coupled ultrasonic transducers (100-500 kHz range)
  • Automated data acquisition for continuous monitoring
  • Standard concrete samples with varied mix designs
  • Parallel mechanical testing (compression, elastic modulus) for correlation
  • Environmental control (temperature, humidity) during curing

Data Analysis

  • Signal processing for wave velocity and attenuation extraction
  • Dispersion curve analysis for depth-dependent property assessment
  • Statistical modeling correlating NDT measurements with strength
  • Machine learning for predictive strength assessment

Key Findings

Research Outcomes:

  • Successfully modeled leaky Rayleigh wave propagation in air-concrete system
  • Demonstrated strong correlation (R² > 0.85) between wave velocity and compressive strength
  • Identified optimal frequency range (200-300 kHz) for early-age monitoring
  • Validated FEA predictions against experimental data within 10% error
  • Developed calibration curves for different concrete mix designs
  • Contributed to NSF proposal on multiphysics modeling of concrete

Applications & Impact

  • Construction Quality Control: Real-time monitoring of concrete strength development
  • Formwork Removal: Objective criteria for safe stripping time determination
  • Cold Weather Concreting: Monitoring hydration in challenging conditions
  • Defect Detection: Early identification of curing anomalies or segregation
  • Research & Development: Rapid assessment of novel concrete formulations
  • Infrastructure Safety: Non-invasive evaluation without damaging structure

Technical Innovations

  • Air-coupled FEA framework for acoustic-structural interaction
  • Time-dependent material models capturing concrete curing kinetics
  • Automated signal processing pipeline for continuous monitoring
  • Calibration methodology adaptable to various mix designs

NSF Proposal Contribution

This research contributed to an NSF proposal on "Multiphysics Modeling of Early-Age Concrete Evolution Using Non-Contact Sensing," addressing critical knowledge gaps in understanding and predicting concrete performance during the critical early curing period.

Future Directions

  • Extension to in-situ field deployment on construction sites
  • Development of portable, wireless monitoring systems
  • Integration with Building Information Modeling (BIM) platforms
  • Application to other cementitious materials (shotcrete, repair mortars)
  • AI-enhanced interpretation for automated quality assessment
